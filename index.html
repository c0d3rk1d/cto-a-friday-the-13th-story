In reality, being a CTO often feels like living in a horror movie.

The Haunted Tech Stack

Legacy Code: The crypt you can't escape

You, new developers, often think you will always build from scratch.

Truth is, you inherit a lot.

CTOs must balance innovation with maintaining legacy systems.

The Fires That Never Die

Incident hell: Every day is Friday the 13th

Production issues happen. Often. And late at night.

CTOs are ultimately accountable, but developers are the frontline heroes.

Stakeholders: The Vampires of Time

The projects that won't stay dead

CTOs spend lots of time translating between business and tech.

You will need to explain technical choices to non-technical people.

The Zombie Projects

Meetings that suck the life out of you

CTOs manage expectations and document decisions.

Track why a project was paused or killed - so history doesn't repeat.

The Dark Magic of Leadership

Wizardry, witchcraft, and wardrobe changes

Leadership is more about people than code.

CTOs mentor, resolve conflict, hire smartly, and protect team focus.

Top 5 Things I Wish I Knew Before Becoming a CTO

Becoming a CTO isn't just about knowing code

it's about navigating chaos, people, and pressure.

If I could go back, here are the five things I wish someone told me.

First

Tech is the easy part — people are the hard part.

Tech is the easy part.

It's the people part that will keep you up at night.

Hiring, culture, communication

Second

You'll write less code but make a bigger impact.

You will write less code, but your decisions will ripple across the entire org.

Your impact scales when you stop being the smartest coder in the room and start empowering others.

Third

Your job changes every 3-6 months.

Your job will change every 3 to 6 months.

New challenges, new fires, new expectations.

Stay adaptable.

Fourth

Document everything. Future-you will thank you.

Document everything.

Future-you — and your team — will thank you when things break at 2 AM.

Finally

Vision without execution is hallucination. Lead both.

And finally, vision without execution is just hallucination.

You need to lead both. Paint the future and ship the damn product!

That's the real job.

If You Want to Be a CTO...
